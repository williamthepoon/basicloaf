Welcome to the Basic Loaf Family!

First of all, thank you for buying a portion of my sourdough starter. You've just skipped the hardest 14 days of the sourdough journey! This starter is mature, active, and ready to help you bake incredible bread.

Because this is a fresh starter (not dried), it's a living culture that needs a little attention depending on how it got to you.

If You Collected Your Starter in Person

Since you picked this up directly, the starter is likely very happy and active.

  • Baking Today? If I gave it to you freshly fed, you can use it as soon as it peaks (doubles in size). Check the Signs of Readiness.
  • Baking Later? If you aren't baking in the next few hours, pop the jar straight into the fridge. It will stay happy there for up to a week before it needs a feed.
  • Maintenance: When you are ready to bake, follow my Scrapings Feeding Method to build up the amount you need.

If You Received Your Starter by Post

Being in a dark box for 24-48 hours can be a little stressful for the yeast. It might look a bit liquid, have a few bubbles, or smell quite "vinegary" or strong. This is completely normal!

  1. Feed Immediately: As soon as you open it, transfer the starter to a clean glass jar. Give it a "1:1:1" feed (e.g., 50g starter, 50g lukewarm water, 50g strong white bread flour).
  2. Let it Wake Up: Leave it at room temperature. It should start bubbling and rising within a few hours.
  3. The "Double Check": I recommend feeding it twice at room temperature before your first bake to ensure it's back to full strength. Once it's reliably doubling in 4-6 hours, it's ready!
  4. Storage: Once active, you can move it to the fridge and follow the standard maintenance in the Starter Guide.

A Few Tips for My Specific Starter

  • It Loves Warmth: This culture is used to a cozy kitchen. If your house is cool, use lukewarm water (35°C) for feedings to keep it moving.
  • Don't Overthink It: Sourdough is resilient. If you forget a feed, just give it some flour and water and it will almost always bounce back.
  • Ready to Bake? Once your starter is bubbly and active, head over to The Recipe to start your first Basic Loaf.
